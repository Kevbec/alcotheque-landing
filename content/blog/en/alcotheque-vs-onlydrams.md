# Alcotheque vs OnlyDrams: Which Spirits App Is Right for You?

If you collect whisky, bourbon, rum, or gin seriously, you have probably come across OnlyDrams. It is one of the most feature-rich spirits collection apps available, with a database of over 56,000 bottles, barcode scanning, community features, and tools built specifically for the spirits world.

Alcotheque takes a different approach — quieter, more private, and built for collectors who want to manage wine and spirits together without the social layer.

This comparison is not about which app is better in absolute terms. Both are genuinely good. It is about which one fits how you actually collect.

---

## What OnlyDrams Is Built For

OnlyDrams describes itself as built by collectors, for collectors. That ethos shows in the feature set. The app launched with a clear focus on the whisky and bourbon community — particularly the American market — and has expanded to cover the full range of spirits categories.

The core strengths are real. A database of 56,000+ bottles with verified pricing data gives collectors a reference point for market value that very few apps can match. The barcode scanner pulls up detailed bottle information instantly. The Bottle Drops feature — direct partnerships with distilleries for release announcements — is genuinely useful for collectors chasing allocated releases. The Infinity Bottle tracker, which lets you document custom blends pour by pour, is something no other mainstream app offers.

OnlyDrams also has a community dimension: giveaways, distillery partnerships, and social features that connect collectors with each other and with brands.

What it does not cover: wine. OnlyDrams is a spirits platform. If your collection includes bottles of Burgundy alongside your Islay single malts, OnlyDrams will not help you with half of what you own.

---

## What Alcotheque Is Built For

Alcotheque was built for collectors who do not want to choose between their wine and their spirits. The inventory is unified — one interface for everything on your shelves, whether that is a Châteauneuf-du-Pape, a Hampden rum, or a bottle of Hendrick's.

The scanning approach is different from OnlyDrams. Rather than looking up a barcode in a pre-built database, Alcotheque uses AI-powered OCR to read the label directly — extracting the name, type, vintage, producer, and other details automatically. This means it works on bottles that are not in any database, including older expressions, small-batch releases, and regional producers with limited distribution.

Privacy is a core design principle. There is no public profile, no community feed, no social layer. Your collection is yours and stays yours — it never becomes a data point in someone else's platform.

The gift tracking feature — logging which bottles you received and from whom, and which you offered as gifts — is something OnlyDrams does not replicate in a dedicated way. For collectors who regularly exchange bottles or receive them as gifts, this matters.

Alcotheque is currently available on iOS, with Android support on the roadmap. It is also available in 8 languages, making it genuinely useful for collectors across Europe and beyond, not just the English-speaking market.

## On Scanning: Two Different Philosophies

The scanning approach is where these two apps diverge most clearly — and understanding the difference helps you choose.

**OnlyDrams uses barcode scanning.** Point the camera at the barcode on the back of the bottle and the app pulls up the entry from its database of 56,000+ verified bottles. This is fast, accurate for bottles that are in the database, and comes with rich data including historical pricing. The limitation: if the bottle is not in the database — an older expression, a craft distillery, a regional producer — the scan may not return a result.

**Alcotheque uses AI label scanning.** Point the camera at the front label and the AI reads it directly, extracting name, type, vintage, producer, and other details. This works on virtually any bottle regardless of whether it exists in a pre-built database. The trade-off: you will not get historical market pricing from the scan, though you can track purchase price and estimated value manually.

Neither approach is universally superior. If you collect mainstream releases from well-known distilleries, OnlyDrams' barcode system is faster. If you collect broadly — including older expressions, small producers, or wine — Alcotheque's label scanning covers more ground.

## Who Should Use OnlyDrams

If you collect spirits exclusively — particularly whisky, bourbon, and allocated American releases — OnlyDrams is an exceptionally well-built tool. The database depth, the Bottle Drops feature, the Infinity Bottle tracker, and the community dimension make it the most feature-complete spirits-specific app available.

It is also the right choice if you need Android support right now, since Alcotheque is currently iOS only — though Android is on the roadmap.

## Who Should Use Alcotheque

If your collection includes wine alongside spirits — even a few bottles — Alcotheque is the only app that handles both in one place. There is no alternative that manages a mixed collection with the same level of detail.

If you collect spirits that are not well-covered by mainstream databases — older expressions, craft producers, regional bottles — the AI label scanning approach works better than barcode lookup.

If you want a [private inventory](https://www.alcotheque.app/en/blog/what-is-a-wine-and-spirits-collection-app) with no social profile and no community dimension, Alcotheque is designed for exactly that. And if you regularly give or receive bottles as gifts, the [gift tracking feature](https://www.alcotheque.app/en/blog/how-to-track-wine-gifts) is something OnlyDrams does not replicate.

For European collectors in particular, the 8-language support and the coverage of wine categories make Alcotheque a more natural fit than a platform built primarily for the American bourbon market.

## Can You Use Both?

Yes. Some collectors use OnlyDrams for its database and release alerts, and Alcotheque for their full inventory including wine. The two apps serve different purposes and do not conflict.

If you are starting fresh and want one app for everything — wine, spirits, gifts, locations — Alcotheque is the simpler choice. The free plan covers 15 bottles and asks for nothing upfront.

## FAQ

### Does OnlyDrams support wine collections?
No. OnlyDrams is focused exclusively on spirits. If your collection includes wine, Alcotheque is the only app that manages both wine and spirits in one unified interface.

### Does Alcotheque have a spirits database like OnlyDrams?
Alcotheque uses AI-powered label scanning to extract bottle details automatically without requiring a pre-built database lookup. OnlyDrams relies on a barcode scanner against its database of 56,000+ bottles.

### Is OnlyDrams free?
OnlyDrams has a free tier with collection management, barcode scanning, database access, and up to 3 custom lists. The Elite plan unlocks custom fields, enhanced pricing, exports, and unlimited lists.

### Which app is better for tracking wine gifts?
Alcotheque. It has a dedicated gift tracking feature for logging bottles received and offered, with context like who gave them and when. OnlyDrams has a gifted status in bottle history but no dedicated gift tracking workflow.

### Can I use Alcotheque if I only collect spirits, not wine?
Absolutely. Alcotheque works perfectly for spirits-only collectors. The wine support is there if you need it, but it never gets in the way of managing a pure spirits collection.
